What Are Puppy Mills?

A commercial facility that breeds dogs in filthy and overcrowded tiny cages for the sole purpose of supply animals to pet shops. Dogs are denied of proper veterinary care and effects of this has been very horrifying.

Where are Puppy Mills?

They are all around you!

Pet Stores

Almost 99.9% of the pets sold in petstores are from puppy mills.

Online

Online transaction of pets are almost always pets from puppy mills.

Advertisment

Advertisement are almost always tied to puppy from puppy mills.

Why Do Puppy Mills exist?

The Federal government (USDA) regulates low standards on breeders. They don't promote responsible breeding to ensure healthy puppies. For example, it's legal for licensed breeders to own 1,000 or more dogs, keep them in very small cages for their entire lives, and breed them as often as possible.

There are very few inspectors in each state to thoroughly inspect for hundreds – sometimes thousands – of licensed breeding facilities all around America. Often many don't get inspected properly.

Pet companies see this as opportunities to make a profit. As a result, a vicious cycle of puppies bred in large numbers and not cared for. Maybe of these puppies develop health problem later on and gets abandoned
